Your experience matters

With Aucultur you can find the bests plans in town, while helping programmers and cultural creators to develop better proposals.

Aucultur is a platform under development which tries to understand how satisfaction is built in a cultural experience. It is a pilot, experimental and multidisciplinary project developed by the Econcult research unit, in close collaboration with the Department of Methodology of Behavioral Sciences of the University of València and PROS (Research Center on Software Production Methods) of the Polytechnic University of València and funded, in its initial phases, by the Valencian Agency for Innovation.

Its aim is to become a democratic tool, to assess the services that cultural or political institutions can adopt, by using an interactive platform that produces big data.

The platform is designed to generate a database that allows understanding, at an aggregate level, how satisfaction is built in the participation of a cultural event. It allows to assess the general impacts on individuals of different cultural events such as concerts, visits to heritage sites, scenic performances, exhibitions or conferences.

The Aucultur platform helps cultural and political organizations to meet the needs and interests of its users, and allows individuals to express their voice and engage in active cultural dynamics in the city.

What is Auculture used for?

The data collected through the platform allows us to:

Investigate the impacts of culture on individuals: analyze the similarities and differences according to the different artistic activities; analyze the reactions of each specific segment of the public; develop comparative research on cultural uses and practices in different communities and territories; study cultural participation from a gender perspective.

Provide information to people who program cultural events to improve knowledge about the audiences which participate in their events.

Improve the adjustment between supply and demand for cultural events.

Create a social network of people who participate in the cultural life of the community to improve the quality and intensity of cultural experiences.

Background

The evaluation of cultural events, in general, has consisted of the judgment of critics who publish in more or less specialized spaces. The number of people attending the cultural event has also been used to evaluate the impact, under the assumption that everyone who has attended has received the same impact and similar level of interest, showing identical levels of satisfaction, or that it affects in a similar way. in terms of recommendation, loyalty and repetition towards the event. These insufficient strategies for knowing the audiences mean that there is little real information on the impacts generated by the attendance of users at cultural events, so that the decisions of the people involved (in the field of creation and/or management and programming) are of poor technical quality and can cause inefficiencies, inclusion problems and lack of compliance with the objectives. Aucultur presents a global approach to measure the impact of cultural participation and the behavior of users of culture, combining socio-psychological and economic approaches. The model combines contingent evaluation techniques with the analysis of individual characteristics and the perception of cultural experiences to evaluate the impacts of cultural participation on individuals. The objective of this model is to allow a complete understanding of the behavior of the individual in the evaluation of the cultural experience. This crossed approach allows to overcome some limitations: the lack of a comprehensive measurement approach, which allows producing a panoramic image of consumption and cultural experience and the difficulty to collect quantitative data related to the socio-psychological dimension with the existing measurement approach.
